What Counts as a Plumbing Emergency?

Not every plumbing problem justifies a midnight call. Here's how to tell the difference:

Call immediately — don't wait until morning:

  • Burst or cracked pipe with water actively flooding your home
  • Sewage backing up through drains, toilets, or floor drains
  • Complete loss of water — main line frozen or broken
  • Water heater leaking heavily with risk of flooding
  • Gas smell near plumbing fixtures (also call the gas company and exit the home)
  • Toilet overflowing continuously and shutoff valve isn't working
  • Active flooding from any plumbing source

Can probably wait until business hours:

  • Slow-draining sink or tub that still empties
  • Running toilet that isn't overflowing
  • Dripping faucet
  • Low water pressure without visible leaking
  • Water heater producing lukewarm water but not leaking

If you're not sure, call anyway. A good plumber will tell you honestly whether it needs immediate attention or can wait — and that phone call doesn't cost anything.

🚨 First Things First

Before you call, shut off your main water valve if there's active flooding. In most Kennebunk homes, it's in the basement near the foundation wall where the water line enters. If your home is on well water, the shutoff is typically near the pressure tank. Turning off the water before the plumber arrives can prevent thousands of dollars in damage.

Kennebunk-Specific Plumbing Challenges

Kennebunk has plumbing quirks that come with its character. A local plumber who knows the area understands these:

  • Historic homes near the village: Many homes along Summer Street, Main Street, and the surrounding blocks date back to the 1800s. They often have galvanized steel or even lead pipes that are more fragile and require careful handling during emergencies.
  • Coastal properties: Homes near Kennebunk Beach, Gooch's Beach, and into Kennebunkport take a beating from salt air and coastal humidity. Exposed plumbing corrodes faster, and freeze risk is higher with ocean wind chills.
  • Seasonal properties: Kennebunk and Kennebunkport have a significant number of vacation homes and summer rentals. If these aren't properly winterized, pipes freeze and burst with nobody home to catch it. We see this every single winter.
  • Mixed water systems: Some Kennebunk homes are on the Kennebunk, Kennebunkport & Wells Water District, while others — especially on the outskirts toward Alewive or West Kennebunk — are on private wells. This affects both the plumbing approach and the parts needed.

What to Do While You Wait for the Plumber

Once you've called, here's how to minimize damage while waiting:

  1. Shut off the main water valve if there's active flooding
  2. Turn off the water heater — if your main water is off, the heater can overheat without flow
  3. Take photos of all damage for your insurance claim
  4. Move valuables away from water — electronics, documents, rugs, anything you can grab quickly
  5. Open windows if there's a sewage smell — ventilation matters for your health
  6. Don't use any drains or toilets during a sewage backup — you'll make it worse

Does Insurance Cover Emergency Plumbing?

In most cases, homeowner's insurance covers sudden and accidental plumbing damage — a burst pipe, an appliance failure, a sudden leak. What it typically doesn't cover is damage from gradual leaks, lack of maintenance, or failure to winterize a vacant property. Document everything with photos before any cleanup starts, and call your insurance company the same day. Our plumbers provide detailed invoices that insurance companies accept for claims.
