What Counts as a Plumbing Emergency?

Call immediately — don't wait until morning — for any of the following:

  • Burst or broken pipe — water actively flooding your home
  • Sewage backup — raw sewage coming up through drains or toilets
  • No water at all — main line frozen, broken, or shut off unexpectedly
  • Gas line smell near plumbing fixtures (also call your gas company)
  • Water heater failure with active leaking — risk of flooding
  • Flooding from any plumbing source
  • Toilet overflow that won't stop
🚨 First: Know Where Your Shutoff Valve Is

Every Maine homeowner should know how to shut off their main water supply. It's usually in the basement near where the water line enters the home, or outside near the foundation. In a true emergency, shutting off water before the plumber arrives can prevent thousands in water damage.

What Can Wait Until Morning?

Not every plumbing issue is a true emergency. The following can usually wait until regular business hours (saving you the after-hours surcharge):

  • Slow-draining sink or tub (not fully blocked)
  • Running toilet that isn't overflowing
  • Dripping faucet
  • Low water pressure (no flooding involved)
  • Minor water heater issues (still producing some hot water)

When in doubt, call and describe the situation — our plumbers can help you decide over the phone whether it needs immediate attention.

What to Do While You Wait for the Emergency Plumber

  1. Shut off the main water valve if there's active flooding or a burst pipe
  2. Turn off the water heater — if your main water is off, the heater can overheat without water flowing through it
  3. Document the damage — photos for your insurance claim
  4. Move valuables away from water — electronics, important documents, rugs
  5. Open windows if there's sewage smell to ventilate
  6. Don't use any drains or toilets during a sewage backup

💡 Does Homeowner's Insurance Cover Plumbing Emergencies?

Often yes — especially for sudden and accidental damage (burst pipe, appliance leak). Slow leaks and maintenance failures are usually not covered. Document everything with photos before any cleanup, and call your insurance company the same day. Our plumbers can provide detailed invoices for insurance claims.
