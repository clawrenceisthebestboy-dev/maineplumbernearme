Signs Your Pipes Are Frozen

  • You turn on a faucet and nothing comes out (or just a trickle)
  • Visible frost on exposed pipes under sinks or in basements
  • Strange smells from your faucet ‚Äî blocked pipes can trap odors
  • Banging or gurgling sounds in your walls when you try to run water
  • Unusually cold spots on walls (especially exterior walls)

If you suspect a pipe has already burst ‚Äî you'll hear rushing water, see wet walls/ceiling, or notice sudden drops in water pressure ‚Äî shut off your main water valve immediately and call a licensed plumber. Every minute counts.

Step 1: Don't Panic ‚Äî Don't Use Open Flame

The most dangerous mistake Maine homeowners make is grabbing a propane torch to thaw frozen pipes. Never use open flame on pipes. It can ignite insulation, damage PVC pipes, and has caused house fires across Maine. A hair dryer, heat lamp, or electric heating tape is safe. A torch is not.

Step 2: Open the Faucet

Before you start thawing, open the faucet that the frozen pipe feeds. This does two things: it relieves pressure buildup (which is what actually causes pipes to burst), and it gives you a way to tell when the pipe is thawed ‚Äî water will start flowing again.

Step 3: Apply Gentle Heat to the Frozen Section

Start from the faucet end and work back toward the cold section. Safe methods include:

  • Hair dryer ‚Äî works well on accessible pipes, keep it moving
  • Electric heating tape ‚Äî wrap around the pipe, plug in, let it work
  • Space heater ‚Äî point at the area, keep it at a safe distance
  • Hot towels ‚Äî wrap around the pipe for light freezes
  • Heat lamp ‚Äî good for basement or crawlspace situations

Step 4: Never Leave the House While Thawing

If a pipe is going to burst, it'll likely happen during the thaw ‚Äî when pressure that's been building gets released all at once. Stay home, watch for water, and know where your main shutoff valve is before you start.

What If You Can't Find the Frozen Section?

If you can't locate the frozen section ‚Äî which is common when pipes run through walls or under floors ‚Äî it's time to call a licensed Maine plumber. Trying to thaw pipes you can't see is how people flood basements and cause far more damage than the original freeze would have.

How to Prevent Frozen Pipes in Maine

Prevention is always cheaper than repair. Here's what Maine plumbers recommend:

  • Let faucets drip on nights below 20¬∞F ‚Äî especially on exterior walls
  • Keep your heat above 55¬∞F even when you're away (a common mistake during vacations)
  • Insulate pipes in unheated spaces ‚Äî basement, crawlspace, garage
  • Seal gaps in walls and foundation where cold air gets in near pipes
  • Open cabinet doors under sinks on exterior walls during cold snaps
  • Know your main shutoff valve location ‚Äî every Maine homeowner should know this by heart

When to Call a Plumber

Call a licensed Maine plumber immediately if:

  • Thawing has been unsuccessful after 30 minutes
